Examples
Zika Analysis

Researchers at Northeastern University ran 10+ milllion simulations to model the Zika virus and drastically reduced the time for analysis by using a cloud platform.

Google Compute Engine

Google Compute Engine was utilized to run and manage 250,000 independent simulations, cutting the costs by 80% with preemptible instances.

Google BigQuery

Google BigQuery was utilized to analyze terabytes of the simulation results in seconds.

Big genomic data for understanding autism

MSSNG, a collaboration between Google and Autism Speaks, applies the power of cloud computing to sequence the DNA of 10,000 families affected by autism.

Google Cloud Storage

Google Cloud Storage was utilized to securely store and share the petabytes of genomic data with the autism research community. The project's plan to upload the whole genome of 10,000 individuals makes it the largest single repository of autism-related DNA sequencing data.

Google App Engine

Google App Engine was used to provide a secure and standardized access to research data via the GA4GH Application Programming Interface (API), enabling seamless collaboration in the autism research community.

Software for germline and cancer genome analysis

The Broad Institute published GATK, the only open-sourced software for all major variant classes for germline and cancer genome analysis, using cloud technology.
